CCBSA & Thuto-Thebe

Coca-Cola Beverages South Africa (CCBSA) recently partnered with the Thuto-Thebe Foundation and Caring for Girls organisation to donate sanitary towels to the value of R120 000 for learners. Her Royal Highness, Princess Tirelo Molotlegi is spearheading this campaign through their foundation Thuto-Thebe, which aims to improve the quality of education in South Africa for a more sustainable future. The launch held at the Kutlwanong School of the Deaf resulted in over 250 learners from their school and learners from surrounding schools receiving sanitary towels. The Caring for Girls foundation was formed by Richard Mabaso who took up this worthy cause to provide girls with much needed sanitary towels from an underprivileged background.

Starlight Global, a Bahamas-based charity, held a "Night Of Inspiration" in Johannesburg this month. Guests were treated to a delicious dinner with Sibongile Mngoma and a string quartet. Andbank, a family-owned Fitch-rated A-boutique private bank, sponsored the event and made a donation to the Joburg Ballet Company. Juan Iglesias, CEO of Andbank (Bahamas) Limited, expressed the excitement of visiting the continent for the first time. The event was attended by South Africa's former Central Bank Governor, Emeritus Tito Mboweni, Political Analyst Moeletsi Mbeki, Princess Tirelo Molotlegi of the Royal Bafokeng Nation, Ms. Josina Machel, Nkosinathi Biko, NBA's Managing Director for Africa, and other guests from Botswana and Zambia. All guests received a signed copy of Dr. Myles Munroe's latest book and chocolates from Bahamian Chocolatier, Graycliffe. During their stay in Phokeng, they visited the eco-friendly College of the Royal Bafokeng, Lebone II, and the Royal Bafokeng Administration, where the "Masterplan" for 2035 was presented.The current King, Kgosi Leruo Tshekedi Molotlegi, is the 36th recorded King and the 15th in a direct father-son line. How two simple exercises changed my life

Princess Tirelo Molotlegi is a real princess who works with the National Basketball Association to expand a successful after-school NBA basketball/mentoring program in Phokeng, South Africa. For two to three hours a day, five days a week, three retired NBA players mentor a dozen youth, giving them one-on-one attention as well as group training through basketball. The program teaches essential life lessons such as the importance of teamwork, confidence, self esteem, motivation and exercise. Princess Tirelo admits that the kids took such a liking to basketball since the big sports in that region are rugby and soccer. She believes one major factor in the interest and success of the program is the attention, support and love each student receives from the coaches. Princess Tirelo is passionate about inspiring the youth and helping them discover their authentic selves. The NBA program led by her brother, King Kgosi Leruo Molotlegi, has changed the lives of students in remarkable ways. Princess Tirelo and her family have big plans on expanding the program and getting more NBA involvement with the goal of engaging, educating and inspiring these future leaders to discover what makes them authentically special. To help them grow, one can donate to support education in Africa through Thuto Thebe Educational Trust, volunteer their program design or business development skills, or become a strategic partner.
